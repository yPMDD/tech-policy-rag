On the other hand, where the seriousness of the infringement is concerned, the numbers are based on insights from current fining practice and extensive internal testing with fining models over the course of multiple years. The EDPB is convinced that these starting points do justice to the principles of effectiveness, proportionality and dissuasiveness, as prescribed in Article 83(1) GDPR. - However, as always, the EDPB is mindful that the calculation of an administrative fine is no purely mathematical exercise, and that real life cases and practice will inevitably lead to a further sharpening of the starting points in this table. To that end, the Guidelines mention that the table and the numbers therein remain under close review by the EDPB and will be adapted if needed. - It should also be reiterated that these figures are the starting points for further calculation, and not fixed amounts (price tags). The supervisory authority has the discretion to utilise the full fining range from any amount up to and including the legal maximum. - In the first step, the more serious the infringement is within its own category, the higher the starting amount is likely to be. - In the second step, the percentages are leading in determining the final starting amount, whereas adjustments can be made down to a certain percentage of the starting amount identified in step 1. This means that the percentage chosen in step 2 shall be used as a multiplier to the starting amount determined in step 1. The higher the turnover of the undertaking is within its applicable tier, the higher the starting amount is likely to be in step 2. - The amounts in the second step illustrate merely the lowest of lows and the highest of highs that can be applied in the category. The final starting amount will fall somewhere within these extremities. These ranges in the second step therefore serve as a sanity check for the case handler. - Note that there is no adjustment in step 2 for undertakings with a turnover of 500 million euros and higher, since those undertakings will exceed the static legal maximum and, thus, the size of the 85 Commission Recommendation of 6 May 2003 concerning the definition of micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (notified under document number C(2003) 1422), (2003/361/EC). --- PAGE 44 --- Adopted undertaking is already reflected in the dynamic legal maximum used to determine the starting amount for further calculation in step 1. - The application of the methodology, including the use of the tables, is illustrated by two examples at the end of this Annex. For the purposes of this example, the company only has infringed Article 9 of the GDPR. The supervisory authority, after having analysed all relevant circumstances in the case under Articles 83(2)(a), (b) and (g), decided that the infringement is of a high level of seriousness. After this, the supervisory authority has to decide on the starting amount for further calculation. Article 9 is listed in Article 83(5)(a) GDPR, which states that the legal maximum is either €20 million or 4% of the annual turnover. In this case, the turnover of the undertaking is less than €500 million, which means that the static maximum and range is applicable. Therefore, a starting amount between 20 and 100% of the applicable legal maximum, i.e. €4 million and €20 million, should be considered. Considering that the more serious the infringement is within its own category, the higher the starting amount is likely to be, the supervisory authority decides that the starting amount based on the seriousness of the infringement as noted in step 1 should be €10 million. In step 2, the starting amount identified in step 1 will be adjusted based on the size of the undertaking. The undertaking has an annual turnover of €200 million and therefore falls within the range between €100 million and €250 million. This means that the starting amount will be adjusted to an amount between 15% and 50% of the starting amount. Considering that the higher the turnover of the undertaking is within its applicable tier, the higher the starting amount is likely to be, the supervisory authority decides that an adjustment down to 40% of the starting amount identified in step 1 is justified based on the size of the undertaking. The starting amount after the adjustment will then be€4 million in this case. To ensure that this starting amount is in line with the Guidelines, it is possible to cross-check this amount against the ranges included in the applicable table. Considering that Article 83(5) GDPR is applicable, the undertaking has a turnover between €100 million and €250 million and the level of seriousness is high, the starting amount should be between €600,000 and €10 million.
